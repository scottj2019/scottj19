For our second rotation we worked on Autodesk Fusion 360 to create objects we could later have in real life using 3D printing.

The first challenge of this rotation functioned as an introduction to the Autodesk application. Fortunately for myself I've used the app before, and others like it, so I didn't have much trouble with the challenge. We simply had to reacreate an object on the computer with only a picture for reference.

There was a second part to the first challenge in which we were given a catalog of about 50 different desings, and we had to replicate at least five of them in addition to the one given to us on paper. While working through these designs I did learn different ways to fillet and chamfer edges that will be useful in the future.

The final challenge of the rotation was to choose one of four tasks to construct something on Autodesk, and actually print or laser cut it in real life. I decided to make a laptop stand just because I have a laptop, but no stand. I watched an online tutorial to build it, and the guy explaining the process actually gave a lot of useful tips and trick of Autodesk that I didn't know before, so I'm glad I chose the stand. I've included an image of the stand that I made on Autodesk, and another one of the stand in real life.
